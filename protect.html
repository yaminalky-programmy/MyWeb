How AI Shield works

Three layers of adversarial perturbation, all within human perceptual limits.

Sinusoidal interference

Structured patterns are added across the image in frequency space. These cause AI models to misread the low-level features they use to identify style, content, and authorship.

Gaussian noise injection

Random Gaussian-distributed perturbations are applied at the per-pixel level. Combined with structure, they push the image's feature-space embedding away from the original, disrupting style-copy AI.

High-frequency dithering

A high-frequency checkerboard-phase dither layer targets the convolutional filters in the first layers of neural networks — the same filters that detect edges, textures, and brush strokes.

Beta disclaimer

This tool applies mathematically sound adversarial perturbations but does not run a live gradient attack against a specific AI model the way Glaze's research implementation does. For maximum protection on artwork intended for galleries or high-value publication, combine MyWeb AI Shield with Glaze. Always save your originals.
